📝 How the Real USCIS Interview Works

  1. A USCIS officer asks you questions verbally from a random set of 20 from the 128-question bank.
  2. The officer stops when you answer 12 correctly (PASS) or 9 incorrectly (FAIL).
  3. Think of your answer in your head, flip the card, then honestly mark right or wrong.
  4. The 65/20 Senior Test uses a pool of 20 special questions, asks 10, and requires 6 correct to pass.

💡 How to Use This Study Guide

  1. Flashcards (In Order): Start here. Go through all 128 cards in sequence.
  2. Random Practice: Once comfortable, use Random mode to test yourself unpredictably.
  3. Simulate Test: Take the simulated test when you feel ready.
  4. Focus on Weak Areas: Use the category filter to drill specific topics.
  5. Senior Test: If you qualify for the 65/20 exemption, focus on the ⭐ starred questions.
  6. Verify Dynamic Answers: Some answers change with elections. Check the FAQ tab.

🧠 Memory Tips

Say answers out loud — this is an ORAL test! Practice speaking clearly. Record yourself and play it back.

📅 Study Schedule

Study 15–20 minutes daily for 4–6 weeks. Spaced repetition beats cramming every time.

🔢 Numbers to Memorize

128 total · 20 asked · 12 to pass · 9 fail · 100 senators · 435 representatives · 27 amendments · 9 justices

⭐ Senior Exemption (65/20)

If you're 65+ and permanent resident 20+ years, study only 20 questions. Look for ⭐. Need 6/10 to pass.

🗣️ Language Options

Qualifying for the 50/20 or 55/15 exemption allows you to take the civics test in your language.
